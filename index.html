What is Musical Theatre?


Musical Theatre is a combination of music, theatre and dance.

The history is commucicated through words, music and movement, conveying humor, love, anger, among others. Musical Theatre works are often called musicals, since the early 20th century. During the 19th century, modern musical theatre was born, with elements established by the works of:
  • Gilbert and Sullivan - W. S. Gilbert (1836-1911) the dramatist and Arthur Sullivan (1842-1900) the composer;
  • Edward Harrigan (1844-1911) - also known as Ned Harrigan, was an actor, singer, dancer, playwright, lyricist and theatre producer;
  • Tony Hart (1855-1891) - Anthony J. Cannon, was an actor, comedian and singer.
Gilbert and Sullivan worked together between 1871 and 1896 in fourteen comic operas among them are H.M.S Pinafore, The Pirates of Penzance and The Mikado. Edward Harrigan and Tony Hart, worked together as Harrigan & Hart, forming one of the best musical theatre partnerships in the 19th century. With this partnership, Harrigan became one of the founders of modern American musical theatre.


"The Black Crook"

"The Black Crook" is considered the first musical theatre, inaugurated on September 12, 1866 in Nibo's Garden on Broadway in New York. "The Black Crook" had 474 performances, one tour, revived on Broadway in 1870-71, 1871-72 and many more.
